S1--阳关三叠 A parting tune with a thrice repeated refrain

This is a Chinese Art Song from a poem in Tang Dynasty(701 D.C.). The lyrics are repeated thrice with little changes in each time with different emotions. The general meanings are as follows:

Morning rain in Wei City wets dust thin.
Refreshing willow twigs and the green inn.
O, friend, please drink another cup of wine.
No one greets you at west of the borderline.

S2-- Parto, parto

This is an Aria for Alto from Act I in La clemenza di Tito (KV 621) composed by Wolfgang Amadeus Mozart. This aria is famous for the extensive range and the dramatic variation of speed.

The meanings of the lyrics are as follows:
I go, I go, but, my dearest, make peace again with me.
I will be what you would most have me be, do whatever you wish.
Look at me, and I will forget all and fly to avenge you;
I will think only of that glance at me.
Ah, ye gods, what power you have given beauty!

Cadanza starts at about 4'21''

S3--The Hun-Duan Mountain

This is a Chinese Folk Song for Alto, chorus and orchestra. The tunes are in Chinese pentatonic scales.

The meanings of the lyrics are as follows:
The mountain road of Hun-Duan Mountain is really rugged.
The sky seems to be like fire, and the water seems to be like silver.
